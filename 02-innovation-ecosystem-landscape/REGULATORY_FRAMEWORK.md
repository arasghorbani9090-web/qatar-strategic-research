# Qatar Regulatory Framework — Applied Research Delivery

*Reference document on entity structures, vendor classification, bid-bond economics, and taxation as they apply to applied-research delivery in Qatar. Sourced from Qatar Ministry of Commerce and Industry, the Qatar Tenders and Bids Regulatory Law (Law No. 26 of 2005), Qatar General Tax Authority (Dhareeba), Qatar Financial Centre, and current professional commentary as of May 2026.*

## Entity structures for applied-research delivery

| Structure | Eligibility | Setup cost (indicative) | Setup time | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sole Proprietorship Company (SPC) | Qatari nationals and GCC nationals only | low | weeks | Local nationals offering professional services |
| Foreign-owned mainland LLC | Open to foreign nationals; 100% foreign ownership permitted in IT, consulting, software services, fintech, and most economic sectors under recent reforms | QAR 15,000–25,000 first year (CR + Chamber of Commerce + premises license + PRO fees) | 8–12 weeks via MOCI | Diversified service mix, public-sector tendering |
| Qatar Financial Centre (QFC) entity | Open to foreign nationals; 100% foreign ownership | USD 5,000 incorporation + USD 5,000/year renewal | ~2 weeks for individual incorporation | Fintech, regulated activities, faster international invoicing |
| Branch of foreign company | Open to foreign companies | varies | varies | Established foreign firms with existing legal entity |

Reforms under the Foreign Investment Law have opened 100% foreign ownership in most economic sectors including IT, consulting, software, and industrial manufacturing. This means foreign nationals operating in applied-research and software services can establish wholly owned LLCs without a Qatari shareholder.

## Trade license (Tarakheesh / Municipal License)

Issued by the Ministry of Municipality after Commercial Registration. For software and consulting activities, a standard commercial-premises license tied to the CR is sufficient. Renewable annually. Government fee: ~QAR 500.

## Activity classification

The Commercial Registration is the gating document for procurement and tender eligibility. Activity codes relevant to applied-research delivery include:

- Computer Programming Activities (ISIC 6201)
- Information Technology Consultancy (ISIC 6202)
- Information Service Activities (ISIC 6311)
- Management Consultancy Activities (ISIC 7022)
- Research and Experimental Development (ISIC 7210 / 7220)
- Other Professional, Scientific and Technical Activities

The activity codes listed on the CR define which categories of procurement an entity is eligible to bid against on Monaqasat.

## Tax registration on Dhareeba

The General Tax Authority's Dhareeba portal is the single platform for tax registration and filing.

- **Trigger**: registration required within 60 days of Commercial Registration commencement
- **Penalty for late registration**: QAR 20,000
- **Corporate income tax (CIT)**: 10% on net profits attributable to foreign ownership; the Qatari-owned share is exempt
- **Withholding tax**: 5% on payments to non-residents for services
- **VAT**: Qatar has not implemented VAT as of May 2026, despite the GCC framework agreement (UAE, KSA, Bahrain, Oman have implemented; Qatar and Kuwait have not)

## Vendor classification and government tender eligibility

### Monaqasat (state procurement portal)

The unified state procurement portal at https://monaqasat.mof.gov.qa/ requires vendor registration plus a classification certificate from the Ministry of Finance. New entities start in the lowest classification tier, which is eligible for smaller tenders.

### MOF classification

A graded supplier classification system based on financial capacity, technical capability, and past performance. Vendor classification tiers gate the maximum tender value an entity can bid against. A first-time vendor with no track record is restricted to the lowest tier, which is generally eligible for tenders below QAR 500,000.

### Qatar Foundation supplier portal

A separate registration at https://www.qf.org.qa/supplier-registration is required to bid on Qatar Foundation tenders, even when those tenders cross-list on Monaqasat. Registration is free, and required documents are the same as Monaqasat (CR, tax card, business profile).

### TASMU vendor accreditation

The TASMU Smart Qatar vendor pathway is a multi-step accreditation chain typically requiring 4–8 months:

1. Qatar-registered entity (CR + tax card)
2. Participation in TASMU workshops
3. Registration of interest with Qatar Development Bank
4. Partnership development with TASMU strategic partners
5. QDB accreditation
6. MoTC vendor registration
7. MOF classification certificate

## Bid bonds and performance bonds

Per Law No. 26 of 2005 (Tenders and Bids Regulatory Law) and subsequent amendments:

- **Standard bid bond**: 5% of contract value, as an unconditional bank guarantee from a local Qatar bank, valid 120+ days
- **Performance bond**: 10% of contract value if awarded
- **Bid-bond exemption**: the tender committee may waive the bid bond for tenders related to consultancy services, technical / research / media / sponsorship / marketing services, or tenders below QAR 500,000
- **Local Tenders threshold**: contracts up to QAR 5,000,000 are concluded through Local Tenders procedure

The bid-bond exemption for sub-QAR-500k consultancy / research / technical-services tenders is the single most important regulatory provision for solo and SME applied-research delivery in Qatar, since it removes the bank-guarantee barrier at the tender-bid stage.

## QCB FinTech Sandbox

Qatar Central Bank operates a multi-tiered Regulatory Sandbox for fintech innovation, with a Regular Sandbox track and a fast-track Express Sandbox. Applications are submitted at https://sandbox.qcb.gov.qa/. Sandbox approval is regulatory permission to operate fintech in a controlled environment with real users — it is not a cash grant. Recent approvals include TrustIn (Digital Escrow) and Paywise/Dibsy (Open Banking payment initiation).

## Currency and banking

- Qatari Riyal (QAR) is pegged to USD at approximately QAR 3.64 / USD 1
- Foreign-currency invoicing is permitted for QFC entities
- Mainland LLC invoicing is typically in QAR
